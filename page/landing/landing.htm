Biochemicals Assay Kits

A "Biochemical Assay" is an analytical procedure to detect and quantity cellular processes (e.g. apoptosis, cell signalling) or metabolic reactions. Biochemical Assay are a reliable, routinely used procedure that helps in characterising targets and understanding of biomolecular functions. Here we present the required, high quality tools for qualitatively measuring of various substances and functional activity of biomolecules (e.g. enzymes).

Antibody

An antibody (Ab), Also known as an immunoglobulin (ig), is a large, Y-shaped protein produced mainly by plasma cells that is used by the immune system to neutralize pathogens such as pathogenic bacteria and viruses. The antibody recognises an unique molecule of the pathogen, called an antigen, via the fragment antigen-binding (Fab) variable region.

Elisa Kit

ELISA (Enzyme-Linked Immunosorbent Assay) is a plate-based assay technique designed of detecting and quantifying substances such as peptides, proteins, antibodies, and hormones. Other names, such as enzyme immunoassay (EIA), are also used to describe the same technology. in an ELISA, an antigen must be immobilised an a solid surface and then complexed with an antibody that is linked to an enzyme. Detection is an accomphlised by assessing the conjugated enzyme activity via incubation with a substrate to produce a measureable product. The most crucial element of the detection strategy is a highly specific antibody-antigen interaction.
